About Me

Seeing the problem, shaping the solution, and building it forward.

I am an MBA Candidate at Carnegie Mellon University's Tepper School of Business, with a background in Electrical and Computer Engineering from UC San Diego and seven years of experience at uLab Systems. I began my career as an engineer and grew into a product leader, building healthcare software that operates in real clinical environments and supports doctors in their daily work.

At uLab, I led foundational platform initiatives that shaped the company's core systems. My work included migrating a legacy desktop platform to the cloud, integrating 3D scanning hardware into clinical workflows, and aligning engineering, clinical, and commercial teams to ensure the system held up in practice. These projects were not about shipping features. They were about building infrastructure that could scale, adapt, and endure real world constraints.

You can explore how I approach company-scale product problems in Professional Work, and how I experiment, learn, and build outside of work in Personal Projects. When I am not building, you will often find me on a soccer field, in the water chasing waves, behind a camera, or in the kitchen trying something new. These moments of curiosity and exploration shape how I think and create, and they live in Explorations, where I document what I am learning beyond my day to day work.

If you are building something meaningful and difficult, I would love to connect and explore how I can contribute. Get in touch.

Explorations

Curiosity is how I stay sharp outside of work. Cooking teaches patience and craft. Soccer builds discipline and trust. Surfing trains calm decisions under uncertainty. Photography slows me down and helps me notice what matters. Each section below is a different way I practice these skills.

Soccer

I have played soccer for over 25 years, mostly as a defender. I have captained teams including the UCSD Chinese Team and Overpower, won 3rd place in the U.S. Southwestern CSSA University Soccer Cup in 2017 and champions in the Bay Area.

Photography

Photography helps me notice details I would otherwise miss. I use it to capture light, movement, and small moments that pass quickly. It trains me to slow down, look carefully, and see before acting.

Cooking

Cooking is where I slow down and work with my hands. I enjoy learning techniques, repeating them until they feel natural, and getting a little better each time. It is a quiet practice of patience and craft.

Surfing

I have been surfing for over 11 years, learning to read the ocean through patience and repetition. Along the way, I have taught nine friends how to surf, sharing not just technique but confidence in uncertain conditions. Each session is a reminder to wait, commit, and adapt.
